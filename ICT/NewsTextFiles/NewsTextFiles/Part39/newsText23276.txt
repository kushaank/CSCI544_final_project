BAE Systems has created a prototype of its armored multi-purpose vehicle. The company is expected to submit a bid on a nearly $8-billion contract to make a new armored vehicle for the Army. .

With $8 billion at stake, two of the largest defense contractors - each with offices in Macomb County - are battling to make the Army's next large combat vehicle.

But the fight - with its own twists and accusations of unfairness - isn't just over which company will be selected to replace a decades-old fleet of 2,900 armored personnel carriers. It's also about picking between wheels and treads and deciding what makes the most sense for a modern Army.
