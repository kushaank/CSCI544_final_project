HUNTSVILLE, Alabama - U.S. Rep. Mo Brooks is seeking a third term in Congress and his challenger is a decorated veteran of the Vietnam War.

Jerry Hill received both the Silver Star and Bronze Star, among other awards, during a 20-year career in the U.S. Army.

While Brooks and Hill are vying for the Republican Party nomination to represent the 5th Congressional District, it's unknown if the GOP nominee will face opposition in the November general election. Mark Bray and Reggie Hill are collecting signatures to qualify to be on the ballot as an independent candidate.

Education: Undergraduate degree from Duke University with double major in history and economics, law degree from the University of Alabama.

Family: Wife Martha, four children and four grandchildren.

Occupation: U.S. Congressman and lawyer.

Issues: The federal deficit, which Brooks has described as the nation's greatest threat, as well as protecting federal jobs in north Alabama.

Education: Bachelor of Arts in business administration at Campbell College in North Carolina, master's in administrative science from the University of Alabama in Huntsville.

Family: Wife Brigitte, children Jerry W. Hill Jr., 50, Gloria Ray, 48, and Sheila, deceased.

Occupation: Retired from U.S. Army Aviation and Missile Command at Redstone Arsenal, working in a variety of positions.

Issues: Restoring the economy, which Hill said should be the biggest priority of Congress, and protecting federal jobs in north Alabama as well as working with both sides in Congress.
