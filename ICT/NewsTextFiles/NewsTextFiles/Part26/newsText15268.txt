
Gandhi once said that a country governed poorly by its own people is better than good governance by a foreign power. What he did not admit to was that, once independent, India would benefit from the British legacy of a trained bureaucracy, an international language, and an advanced system for communications, including railways and governance.

Albeit via imperialism, India, similarly to Singapore and Malaysia, is not only politically stable but internationally aware due to established democratic systems and the English language.
