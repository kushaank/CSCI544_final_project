
Some days, he cleans.  Sunday, he rested on his front stoop watching tractors come and go with full loads of debris.  "The town is bare without the trees and everything, but it's still a beautiful town."

A military check point delegates who comes in and out of Beaver Crossing.  The Nebraska emergency management agency assigns work to volunteers.  A Salvation Army truck stationed outside the fire house serves food.  Tuesday, these services will leave and the town's clean up will be left to its residents.

Monday, May 18 a town meeting will be held at the Community Building downtown.  The meeting starts at 6 p.m. and NEMA will address any unanswered questions, which there are still a lot of.
