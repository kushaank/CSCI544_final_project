
Early Tuesday morning, a few hundred election workers will set up primary polling places for voters across Kentucky. There are 41 precincts in Christian County, 13 in Todd and 15 in Trigg. Combined, there are 59,173 registered voters in the three-county Southern Pennyrile who are eligible to cast ballots in the Republican and Democratic primaries.

The polls are open from 6 a.m. until 6 p.m. No one expects long lines at any of the polls. While that makes things a bit easier for voters who have to squeeze in enough time during a work day to drive to their precinct and cast ballots, it also reveals an unfortunate truth about our democracy.
