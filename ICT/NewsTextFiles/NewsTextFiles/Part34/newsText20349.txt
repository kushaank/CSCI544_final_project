
Taipei, May 20 (CNA) President Ma Ying-jeou on Tuesday acknowledged that his administration has not done enough to meet the expectations of the country's young people and promised to address their "anxieties" by allowing them to have greater participation in policy-making.

In a speech at China Medical University, Taichung, Ma also promised to provide affordable housing and a better job environment, among other things.

The speech marked the half-way point of his second four-year presidential term. He came to office on May 20, 2008 after winning his first presidential election.
