Middle East Respiratory Syndrome (MERS) is a viral respiratory illness that is new to humans.

Florida's first confirmed patient with Middle East Respiratory Syndrome Coronavirus (MERS-CoV) has been discharged from an Orlando-area hospital have now testing negative for the virus, the Florida Department of Health announced this morning.

The patient is a health care worker from Saudi Arabia visiting family in Orlando.

Additionally, all health care workers coming in contact with the patient have tested negative for infection. The health department said there is no widespread threat to the public and that travel to Orlando is safe.

MERS is a respiratory illness whose main symptoms are fever, cough, and shortness of breath, according to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. It can also cause gastrointestinal symptoms, like diarrhea and nausea/vomiting, and kidney failure, the CDC reports. It is often fatal.

So far, three U.S. cases have been confirmed.

For more information, call the Florida Department of Health in Orange County at 407-858-1490 or the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention at 800-232-4636.
