
U.S. natural gas supplies are rising faster than expected, according to Bloomberg.

That's easing concerns about a natural gas shortage for heating next winter, which in turn is spurring traders to make more bearish bets on natural gas prices.

Production from shale deposits in the Midwest and Northeast is amping up the supply, Bloomberg reported.

New Mexico's San Juan Basin is primarily a natural gas basin. The Farmington area saw its economy shrink during the recession and experienced a slow recovery as natural gas prices dropped and drilling for natural gas declined.
