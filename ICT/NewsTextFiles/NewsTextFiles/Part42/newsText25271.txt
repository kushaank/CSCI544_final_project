
President Goodluck Jonathan Monday held a closed door meeting  with Security Chiefs and heads of security agencies to take a critical look, as well as review the  fragile security situation of the country.

It was also gathered that the meeting also discussed strategies that had been put in place to ensure safe release of over 200 girls of Government Secondary school in  Chibok, Borno State who were abducted by violent Islamist terror group, Boko Haram.

Monday's meeting came on the heels of the Regional Security meeting on Boko Haram held Saturday and hosted by French President Francois Hollande at Elysee Palace, Paris, France .

Just Sunday, another car bomb by suicide bomber exploded in predominantly Christian area of Sabon Gari, Kano, killing scores of people and injuring many  others.
