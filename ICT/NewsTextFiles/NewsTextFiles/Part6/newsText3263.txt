
Books @ Noon will meet at noon on Wednesday, May 21, at Ames Public Library. Share what you’ve read recently and hear about what others are reading. For more information, call (515) 239-5633.

The Inkspots writing group will meet at 1:30 p.m. on Wednesday, May 21, in the Social Room at Northcrest Community, 1801 20th St., in Ames. Inkspots meets monthly to discuss and critique members’ writing. To join the group, call (515) 239-5633.

A pork tenderloin sandwich night will be from 5 to 6:30 p.m. on Wednesday, May 21, at American Legion Post 37, 225 Main St., in Ames. Enjoy a sandwich and fries for $6. Stay for an evening of Bingo. Proceeds support community programs. Open to the public.

Ames Golden K Kiwanis will meet at 9 a.m. on Thursday, May 22, at First United Methodist Church, 516 Kellogg Ave., in Ames. William R. Clark will present “Return of a Native: The Landscape and Population Ecology of Bobcats in Iowa.” Visitors are welcome.

National Alliance on Mental Illness Central Iowa will hold its monthly support group for family members of individuals living with mental illness at 7 p.m. on Thursday, May 22, at the NAMI office, 416 Douglas Ave., Suite 205, in Ames. For more information, call (515) 292-9400.

Join the Iowa Prairie Network and Story County Conservation for a summer series of evening prairie walks to see rare blooming plants. The first walk will be at 7 p.m. on Thursday, May 22, at Doolittle Prairie, 14249 560th Ave., south of Story City. Free and open to the public. For more information, call (515) 232-2516.
