
A federal judge has dismissed a complaint that was filed last year by a police fundraising group against Texas Attorney General Greg Abbott after his office found the Austin-based nonprofit violated state law through deceptive trade practices.

The Texas State Troopers Association, which consists of commissioned officers and retirees of the Department of Public Safety but is not affiliated with the agency, came under fire by the state for years of spending the majority of its money on telemarketing instead of services to members. Abbott’s office had even issued a “consumer alert” cautioning donors from supporting the organization.
