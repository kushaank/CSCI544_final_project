- Parental Alienation Support and Intervention provides support and help for the prevention, intervention and stopping of Parental Alienation. Parental alienation is a severe form of psychological abuse that usually occurs during a high conflict divorce, but can happen in intact families and by third parties. It involves the deliberate undermining of the relationship between the children and a parent. PAS Intervention's support groups provide 24/7 help and support to victims of parental alienation. For more information, go to www.PAS-Intervention.com or call (203) 770-0318 and ask for Joan.
