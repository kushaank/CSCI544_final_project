
Kyrgyzstan has discussed its potential future membership in the Customs Union,  Elena Kosolapova writes for Trend .

Kyrgyz Economic Minister, Temir Sariyev, while on a visit to Astana discussed Kyrgyzstan' s entry into the Customs Union with Kazakhstan's Deputy Prime Minister, Bakytzhan Sagintayev, the Kazakh prime minister's official website announced on May 16.

The two sides discussed the roadmap approved by the Kyrgyz government earlier this week for the country's entry into the CU, which consists presently of Belarus, Kazakhstan and Russia .

Moreover they also considered prospects for trade and investment cooperation, including the operation of customs posts, interaction in agriculture and water management, immigration, the 'epizootic situation,' veterinary and phyto-sanitary security.
