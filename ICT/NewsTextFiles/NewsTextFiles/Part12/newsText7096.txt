
The Malawian government said Monday that it has not declared May 20 a public holiday despite it being Election Day.Secretary for Local Government and Rural Development Kester Kaphaizi said in the capital Lilongwe that the general public should know that although Tuesday was Election Day it was not a public holiday.

“We would like to appeal and encourage all employers across the country to make arrangements and allow their employees and subjects to go and vote on the day,” he said.

He, however, wished Malawians a peaceful, free and fair tripartite election during which they would vote for president, Members of Parliament and local councillors.
