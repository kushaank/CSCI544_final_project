WELLINGTON, New Zealand--Kiwi Income Property Trust Ltd. (KIP.NZ) on Tuesday reported a lower full-year net profit on internalization costs.

The company reported an after-tax profit of 101.3 million New Zealand dollars (US$87 million) in the full year to March 31, down 7.7% on the year. The net profit takes into account property and interest-rate-derivative revaluations, insurance income and the cost of terminating the previous management arrangements.

It said unit holders will receive a full-year cash distribution of 6.40 New Zealand cents a unit, in line with guidance.

The company said, "The New Zealand economy is growing positively, as is business and consumer confidence." As a result, it is projecting a full-year cash distribution of 6.50 New Zealand cents a unit for the year to March 31, 2015.
