
Indian media reports ahead of Cabinet formation, Prime Minister-elect Narendra Modi today held discussions with his close aide and Bharathiya Janatha Party general secretary Amit Shah and senior party leader Arun Jaitley even as newly-elected BJP MPs met senior functionaries in New Delhi. Modi held the meeting at the Gujarat Bhawan in Chanakyapuri with Shah and Jaitley, who is likely to be inducted in the Cabinet despite losing in the Lok Sabha election.

The Indian daily 'The Hindu' reported Modi also held a flurry of meetings with former Leader of Opposition in Lok Sabha Sushma Swaraj, Uma Bharti, Varun Gandhi and several newly-elected MPs earlier. The portfolio of finance or external affairs is most likely to be awarded to Arun Jaitley. Chairman of Bharathiya Janatha Party Rajnath Singh's name has been proposed for the Minister of Home Affairs. However, it is been reported that Sushma Swaraj who was the former opposition leader of the Lok Sabha is expecting one of the portfolios of defence, home affairs, finance or external affairs.
