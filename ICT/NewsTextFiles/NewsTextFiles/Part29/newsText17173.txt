
Rome, May 19 - Premier Matteo Renzi on Monday said the coming week will be "key" for Italy's schools.      In a post on his Twitter feed, Renzi said that money will be earmarked for construction of schools, with mayors joining the national government in making this a priority.      Renzi has previously said investments in schools were important to his government and he has been visiting schools about once a week around the country.      In a recent letter to mayors, Renzi said that school construction has been "one of the key points of the agenda of the government from day one".
