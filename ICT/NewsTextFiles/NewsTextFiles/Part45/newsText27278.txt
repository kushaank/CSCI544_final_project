
University of Texas System regents are dipping more deeply into the state’s higher education endowment than their investment policy and money managers recommend. And the regents seem poised on Tuesday to dip even deeper in response to Gov. Rick Perry’s call to hold the line on tuition.

The board of directors for the University of Texas Investment Management Co. voted May 7 to recommend that the regents draw $659 million from the Permanent University Fund for the budget year that starts Sept. 1.
