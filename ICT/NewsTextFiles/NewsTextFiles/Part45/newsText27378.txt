AAA Michigan said on Monday that it expects drivers to shell out less for gas by the Memorial Day holiday weekend thanks to rising supplies. The agency predicted that 36 million Americans will travel at least 50 miles for the occasion this year.

AAA said unexpected developments, such as refinery problems or global conflicts, could comprise the anticipated price relief.

Fuel prices in 2009 increased in the days leading up to Memorial Day weekend but declined during the same period between 2010 and 2012, according to an earlier MLive.com report .

The national AAA Daily Fuel Gauge Report on Monday indicated that the average price for regular, unleaded fuel in the Muskegon area was 6 cents higher than last week's average cost , or $3.73. A year ago from Monday, the average price for gas was $3.88.

The statewide average on Monday was $3.74, while the national average – $3.65 — remained the same as it was last week. The federal average a year ago from Monday, May 19, was also $3.65.

User-submitted reports on GasBuddy.com Monday afternoon indicated that prices were falling in the Lakeshore region. In the city of Muskegon, prices ranged from $3.60 to $3.79, while in Grand Haven, costs were between $3.61 and $3.76.

Prices ranged from $3.66 to $3.68 in Hart and Whitehall.
