
About 50,000 cannabis seeds have been seized during major drug operation in north Queensland.

Police in the state's north last week executed search warrants across the Townsville, Cairns and Mareeba regions.

They allege to have found about 50,000 cannabis seeds and about $175,000 worth of stolen property, including a tractor, farm machinery and weapons.

The drug haul was a result of a 16-month large-scale operation codenamed Kilo Enhance.

Seven men from across north and far north Queensland have been charged with more than 100 offences, including possessing or supplying a dangerous drug and weapons and property-related crimes.

The men are all due to appear in the Townsville or Mareeba magistrates courts in the coming months.
