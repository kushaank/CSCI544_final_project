
On Wednesday from 9 a.m. to 5 p.m., the Greenwich Arts Council will sponsor a round-trip bus rip from Greenwich to Storm King in Mountainville, N.Y. Gourmet box lunches, entrance fees and guided tour of the center are included. The Storm King Art Center is a leading sculpture park with more than 100 large-scale works on 500 acres overlooking the Hudson River. The cost is $150; space is limited. For reservations, call 203-862-6754.

The 50th Jim Fixx Greenwich Memorial Day Road Race will be held on May 26 at 8:15 a.m. beginning at Greenwich Senior Center , with a children's half-mile Fun Run to take place at 9:15 a.m. on the Havemeyer Field track behind Town Hall. Pre-register online at www.threadsandtreads.com until Saturday at 5:30 p.m. and at Threads and Treads, 17 East Putnam Ave., during store hours Monday through Friday 9:30 a.m. to 6 p.m.; Saturday, 9:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m.; and Sunday, noon to 4 p.m. Race material may also be picked up starting Thursday at Threads and Treads.  Registration forms are also are available at the Parks and Recreation Department office at Town Hall, 101 Field Point Road; the Greenwich Civic Center in Old Greenwich and the Bendheim Western Greenwich Civic Center.  In addition, runners can register on May 26 from 6:45 to 7:45 a.m. only, at the Greenwich Senior Center. Pre-registered runners can pick up race materials at that time. Children may register at the track from 8:30 to 9 a.m., prior to the half-mile run.

On Friday at 3:30 p.m., "Hooked on Llamas," with Debbie Elias of The Country Quilt Llama Farm, a program for children 4 and older, will be held at Cos Cob Library , 5 Sinawoy Road, featuring a live llama and discussion of llamas and their uses. Elias will share a llama book, and there will be time for questions and answers, and a chance for all to pet the llama. Weather permitting, the program will be held outdoors. Limited to 30 children. Registration is required at 203-622-6883.

The Old Greenwich Farmers Market opens on May 28 at the Presbyterian Church of Old Greenwich , 38 West End Ave. Market hours are 2:30 to 6 p.m. every Wednesday through mid-November. Rain or shine. The market promotes Connecticut farming and food providers. For more information, visit www.oldgreenwichfarmersmarket.com.

Putnam Ave . Meet new people, hear fresh ideas and become part of a membership focused on staying safe and independent in their own homes. For reservations, call 203-422-2342.

Greenwich Reform Synagogue's Rabbi Andrew Sklarz will offer a weekly study class, "Exploring the Torah," on Thursday evenings at 7 p.m. at Greenwich Reform Synagogue, 1037 E. Putnam Ave . The class will focus on the teachings and traditions that have been passed down from Sinai to the present day. No previous Jewish study experience or Hebrew required for this free program. To register, call 203-629-0018 or visit www.grs.org .
