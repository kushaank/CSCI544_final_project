One Huntsville is a free, informal monthly gathering of people interested in connecting the diverse cultural, racial and religious communities in the Huntsville area. The meeting Tuesday will feature speakers from the Smart Energy Council , a collaboration of community, faith-based and business organizations and businesses in Huntsville that is working to alleviate energy costs through a multi-faceted strategy of weather-proofing, smarter energy use and targeted assistance. The Smart Energy Council was organized by the non-profit Nexus Energy Center in Huntsville.

One Huntsville, which is coordinated by Huntsville's  Interfaith Mission Service , is free and open to the public. The meeting on May 20 will be held in the  University Center Exhibit Hall, with a large parking lot on Ben Graves Drive at the heart of the older part of the campus of the University of Alabama in Huntsville. The program is free and open to the public. Visitors are welcome to bring their own supper (both the UAH cafeteria and café in the University Center are closed for the summer.

One Huntsville meets from 5 to 7 p.m. People are invited to gather for informal conversations from 5 until 5:45 p.m. Community announcements begin at 5:45 p.m., with the program starting at 6 p.m. including time for questions and answers at the end.
