
Ella Grant, the mother of David Lokeith Worley, who died in July after his car ran off the road in Harnett County, holding a family photo of her son. At first, the medical examiner ruled his death an accident, saying he died from trauma due to the car crash. But the medical examiner failed to see what was later discovered by funeral home workers - stab wounds in Worley's back. Worley's widow has now been charged with murder in his death.

Kathy Wilson arranges angels on the marker of her husband's grave in Andrews, N.C. She had to pay to have her husband Jim's body exhumed because the medical examiner made the wrong ruling in his death. An autopsy showed James Wilson died of injuries sustained in a car wreck -- not of a heart attack. She said one of the hardest things she has ever done was to have her husband's body exhumed. It was the only way to prove a state medical examiner's mistake.
