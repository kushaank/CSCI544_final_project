Recurring downpours embedded in thunderstorms dumped 1.06 inches of rain in Portland on Sunday , breaking the record for the date of .59 of an inch set in 2005. Vancouver picked up .76 of an inch, tying the record for the date set in 1957.

A weather-spotter reported a waterspout over the ocean near Lincoln City at noon Sunday, and wind brought down a tree onto two cars in downtown Portland at 4 p.m. Sunday.

Thunderstorms rumble through Portland Sunday Thunderstorms packing lightning, high winds and heavy rain moved through Northwest Oregon Sunday.

High pressure builds back in over the region Tuesday , with temperatures warming into the 70s and into the low 80s Wednesday and Thursday.

Thursday looks to be the warmest day of the week, with a forecast high of 82. A weak weather system moves through Friday, but forecasters expect only a slight chance of showers and mostly in the Cascades.

So far, the beginning of the three-day Memorial Day weekend looks to be cloudy and cool before high pressure returns possibly late Sunday into Monday, according to forecasters with the National Weather Service in Portland .
