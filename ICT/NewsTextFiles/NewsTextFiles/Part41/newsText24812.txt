
SAN FRANCISCO (MarketWatch) — Apple Inc. rose Monday to reclaim the $600-mark in the wake of a deal with Google Inc. to settle all lawsuits, while AT&T slid following the announcement of its multi-billion DirecTV buyout deal.

Apple AAPL, +0.22%  shares gained 1% to $603.26, the first time the stock has traded above $600 since it successfully breached that level in early May. Its previous foray above $600 was in October 2012.

The move higher comes after Apple and Google agreed to dismiss all outstanding patent disputes although there is no cross-licensing agreement as part of the deal.

AT&T Inc. T, +0.03%  shares fell 1.2%. The company said Sunday it agreed to buy satellite-television provider DirecTV for $67.1 billion including current debt. The $95-a-share deal is expected to close in about 12 months and has equity value of $48.5 billion. AT&T said it would finance the transaction through cash, asset sales and debt-market transactions, adding that it would sell its interest in América Móvil SAB to ease the regulatory approval process. DirecTV DTV, +1.09%  shares shed 1.8%.

TripAdvisor Inc. TRIP, +1.76%  shares gained 5.2%. The online travel site is scheduled to present at J.P. Morgan’s technology and media conference on Tuesday.

Johnson Controls Inc. JCI, +0.91%  shares rose 3.9%. The maker of car batteries and automotive seating plans to spin off its automotive-interiors business into a new entity majority-owned by a Chinese partner, according to a Wall Street Journal report.

Campbell Soup shares are falling after on a revised forecast.

U.S.-listed shares of AstraZeneca PLC AZN, +1.15%  sank 13%. The U.K. pharmaceutical company on Monday rejected yet another improved takeover offer from Pfizer Inc., this time valued at roughly $120 billion, or 55 pounds ($92.51) a share. AstraZeneca said it rebuffed the offer, which Pfizer said would be its last, because it undervalues the company. Shares of Pfizer PFE, -0.03%  are up 0.8%.

Nordstrom Inc. JWN, +0.45%  shares dropped 2.8%. Nordstrom is slated to pay quarterly dividend of 33 cents a share next week.

American Electric Power Co. AEP, +0.78%  declined 2.9%. It was not immediately apparent what is dragging on the shares but the stock is up more than 9% year to date.

CPB: Campbell Soup CPB, +0.59%  shares fell 2.4%. The food company on Monday cut its full-year outlook as it reported fiscal third-quarter earnings, now expecting sales from continuing operations to increase about 3% versus earlier estimates of 4% to 5%. The company also said adjusted earnings for the full year would come in at the low end of its current range of $2.53 a share to $2.58 a share.

GOOG: Shares of Google GOOG, -0.41% GOOGL, -0.33%  climbed 1.5%. The Internet giant has held early talks with Twitch, a service that provides live video streaming, regarding a potential acquisition, according to The Wall Street Journal .

TXN: Texas Instruments Inc. TXN, -1.35%  shares rose 1.4%. The stock was upgraded to outperform from sector perform and its price target hiked to $57 from $49 at RBC Capital Markets.
