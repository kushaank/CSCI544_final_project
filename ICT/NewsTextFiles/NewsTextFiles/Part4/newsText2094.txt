
MBABANE – Despite reports by over five South African media houses that construction of the Swaziland-South African rail link was pushed to 2017, Swaziland Railway insists 2017 remains the date for completion of its operations.

Moneyweb, Business Day, Engineering News, Business Report, and Fin24 all reported that the start of construction work was pushed to 2017 as feasibility studies were still going on.

Sive Manana, Corporate Communications Specialist said: “As stated during the press briefing on the project at Mountain Inn on the November 15, 2013, the project is still at stage 3, which is the feasibility stage. This feasibility stage is expected to be complete by August 2014.
