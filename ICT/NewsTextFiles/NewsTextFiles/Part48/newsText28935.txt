
Nepali Congress vice-president Ramchandra Poudel has said that the Sushil Koirala-led government should work by giving priority to the people's agendas.

At a programme on Sunday, Poudel said that country can progress only if the Nepali Congress was stronger and asked the party leaders to be principally equipped.

On the occasion, Poudel said that the far-west region has immense potential in agriculture, tourism and hydropower and for this investment-friendly environment should be created.

On a separate context, Poudel said that India's Nepal policy would not be changed after change in government in India. "Development activities carried out in India will have positive effects in Nepal's economy," he added.
