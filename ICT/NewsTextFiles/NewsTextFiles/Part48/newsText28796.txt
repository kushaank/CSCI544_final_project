
Erlanger police said the man accused in a fatal hit-and-run was arraigned in Kenton County Monday.

A Duke Energy worker found Lauren Pettit, 29, of Erlanger, dead in the grass alongside the roadway in the 2800 block of Crescent Springs Pike across from Tewes Poultry Farm about 8:30 a.m. Saturday.

Officers said they identified Lonnie Turner, 38, as the suspected driver who hit Pettit and did not stop for help.

He was booked into the Kenton County Jail.

Turner was charged with manslaughter and persistent felony offender.

Investigators also said they believe Turner was under the influence of drugs or alcohol at the time of the accident but they would not say what led them to that conclusion.

Officers said it was common for people to walk along that stretch of road between Erlanger and Crescent Springs.

Ther personality of Petti is reflected in the words and images left on her Facebook page where some friends last heard from her.

"Lauren was so beautiful on the inside and out. She was an amazing person who didn't deserve to die in this way."

Turner will be arraigned in Kenton County Court Monday morning.

Turner’s criminal record includes forgery, trafficking cocaine and identity theft.

Turner is being held on $100,000 bond.
