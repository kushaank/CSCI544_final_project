
A New Britain man whom federal authorities believe was the leader of a Hartford heroin trafficking ring with tentacles in the suburbs was sentenced Friday to more than 19 years in federal prison, U.S. Attorney Deirdre M. Daly announced.

The man, Angel Rosa, 37, was known to associates as “Little” and “Daddy” but sometimes called himself “Boss,” according to a sentencing memorandum by prosecutor Brian P. Leaming.

“Little was physically imposing, a known member of the Los Solidos street gang, who ruled his organization through fear and intimidation,” the prosecutor wrote.
