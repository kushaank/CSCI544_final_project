
The United States has offered its condolences to Laos over a deadly plane crash that killed at least 16 people, including cabinet ministers.

A Laotian air force plane carrying senior government officials crashed in a wooded area on Saturday as it approached an airport in the country's northeast.

The crash reportedly claimed at least 16 lives, including that of Defense Minister Douangchay Phichit and his wife.

"We are deeply saddened to hear the news of a plane crash in Laos, which claimed the lives of Lao Government officials and others on board the flight," read a statement issued y US State Department spokesperson Jen Psaki on Sunday.

"We extend our deepest condolences to the Government of Laos. Our thoughts and prayers are with the families of the victims and with all the people of Laos at this difficult time," the statement added.
