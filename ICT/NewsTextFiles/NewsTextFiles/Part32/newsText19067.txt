BANGKOK, May 20 (Reuters) - Thailand's government is still running the country although the army brought in martial law early on Tuesday and will ensure security, the acting justice minister said.

"It's good that the army is looking after the country's security. However, the government still has full power to run the country," Chaikasem Nitisiri told Reuters.
