
As Boko Haram’s attacks on Borno and Yobe  communities become fiercer, with more   than 15, 700 persons displaced from their original homes, the National Emergency Management Agency (NEMA) at the weekend,  provided relief materials to Chibok community, where over 200 schoolgirls were abducted.

Other  communities that benefited from the relief materials include, Gamboru that was raided by the insurgents, resulting in the loss of lives and destruction of many homes. The others are Jakana and Mainok in Kaga Local Government Area of Borno State, which are located on Damaturu-Maiduguri Road and had come under repeated attacks. The relief items were distributed to about 4, 400 displaced persons.

NEMA  also distributed relief items to about 6, 000 persons displaced by the insurgency in Goniri community and its environs in Gujba Local Government Area of Yobe State.

Director General of the agency, Alhaji Muhammad Sani Sidi, who was represented by the North East Zonal Co-ordinator,  Alhaji Mohammed Kanar,  said the reliefs distribution was approved for the affected communities to provide them with succour while the government tackles the security challenges in the areas.

He pledged the continued support of the Federal Government towards the rehabilitation of the displaced persons after the return of peace in their communities and urged them to remain law-abiding.

On the reliefs delivery to Chibok, where about 5, 300 benefited,  he said it was normal to provide succour to the traumatized community, while the government strives to ensure the safe return of their abducted daughters.

The reliefs include food and household items as well as materials for the peculiar needs of women and children.
