
Shame on the Globe for telling Harvard that it should and can censor unpopular or offensive speech because it is a private university ( “ ‘Black mass’: Don’t feed the trolls,” Editorial, May 14). Although the First Amendment does not apply directly to private colleges, good schools understand that freedom of expression is critical to all campuses, not just those that are government-run. Thus, accrediting bodies consider respect for academic freedom in deciding to accredit a college.

Harvard President Drew Faust was right to refuse to censor those who had planned to put on a black mass. Instead, she spoke about why the planned event was offensive. Students can learn from her speech, while censorship rarely teaches positive lessons.
