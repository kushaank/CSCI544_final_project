Four former executives of the Cadillac Ranch restaurant and bar in Cincinnati have pleaded guilty in federal court to tax charges involving the Internal Revenue Service .

Joel Field and Eric Schilder , both of Marion, Ohio, and Jon Field and Paul Butler , both of Dublin, Ohio, each pleaded guilty to one charge involving failing to pay taxes. The four combined to owe the IRS nearly $800,000, according to court documents and a U.S. Justice Department news release.

Joel Field , 58, pleaded guilty to tax evasion. He was an owner and operator of Cadillac Ranch restaurants, including one that operated at Sixth and Walnut streets in downtown Cincinnati but is now closed. Field admitted to amassing unpaid taxes of more than $130,000 between 1997 and 2001. He later gave the IRS false information and transferred some assets into other family membersâ€™ names to prevent the IRS from seizing them, according to court documents. Field agreed to pay restitution of $349,777, according to the news release. Field faces a maximum sentence of five years in prison and a fine of up to $250,000.

Jon Field , also 58, pleaded guilty to filing false income tax returns from 2006 through 2010, according to court documents. He agreed to pay restitution for the tax loss of $174,458 that he caused from his filings. He also faces maximum penalties of five years in prison and a $250,000 fine.

Schilder pleaded guilty to filing a false income tax return in 2008. He caused the IRS to lose $95,000, according to the news release. He faces a maximum sentence of three years in prison and a $250,000 fine.

Butler pleaded guilty to conspiracy to defraud the United States. He admitted in court to failing to pay taxes of more than $150,000 from 1996 to 2001. He said he then helped created a false promissory note to the IRS. He also faces maximum penalties of five years in prison and a $250,000 fine.
