
These incredible pictures show how a town in Oklahoma is rebuilding itself one year after it was ripped apart by a 210mph tornado.

The two-mile wide twister killed 24 people, including seven children, and left hundreds more injured. Many thousands of homes were destroyed along its 17 mile path.

 So were the town's medical centre, theatre and numerous businesses as the tornado caused £1.3million of damage.

Twelve months on there are new houses replacing those that were left in a pile of rubble. Roads have been cleared of fallen street lights and traffic can flow smoothly again. Construction work has also begun at Plaza Towers elementary schoo l where seven pupils died.
