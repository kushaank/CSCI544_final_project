Oswego, N.Y. -- A North Carolina man was charged Friday with sexually abusing two boys, as young as 7 years old, in Oswego County between 2001 and 2005.

Oswego County Sheriff's deputies arrested William A. Bates-Congdon, 49, of Waxhaw, N.C. in his home in North Carolina. He was charged with multiple counts of criminal sexual acts and sexual abuse, committed in Scriba, they said.

Police said that Bates-Congdon had sexual contact with the first victim, who was 7 years old when the contact began, between 2001 and 2003. He continued to have sexual contact with the boy until he was 9, they said.

For those incidents, Bates-Congdon was charged with first-degree sodomy and first-degree sexual abuse, both felonies.

Bates-Congdon had sexual contact with another boy in 2004 and 2005, police said. The victim was 12 and 13 at the time, they said.

He was charged with first-degree criminal sexual act, second-degree criminal sexual act and first degree sexual abuse, all felonies, for those incidents.

Bates-Congdon was arraigned by Judge Kenneth Adkins in the town of Scriba court. He was remanded to the Oswego County Correctional Facility on $20,000 cash bail or $40,000 bond.

His next court appearance is scheduled for May 22.
