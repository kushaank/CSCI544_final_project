
ABUJA - Justice Danladi Umar, Chairman, Code of Conduct Tribunal (CCT), has called for closer collaboration among anti-corruption agencies, to enhance efficiency in the fight against corruption.

Umar’s call is contained in a statement signed by Mr Ibraheem Al-hassan, CCT spokesman in Abuja.

He made the call at a routine meeting of representatives of anti-corruption agencies.

The CCT boss said the meeting, coordinated by the UN Office on Drugs and Crimes (UNODC), was to enhance synergy among stakeholders.

“This kind of meetings will definitely enhance the activities of anti-corruption agencies in combating corruption in the country.

“The collaboration being established through this meeting will create room for sharing of ideas and identifying each other’s problems,” he said.
