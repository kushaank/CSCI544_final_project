Ionia County, Mich.

Former funeral director Marti Schrauben was found guilty of 26 charges related to racketeering, embezzlement and forgery Monday afternoon. The verdict in the People of the State of Michigan v. Marti Schrauben case was delivered after jury deliberation on Friday and Monday afternoons.

Schrauben, 40, of Portland, was represented by defense attorney Mike Hocking, and the prosecution was led by Assistant Attorney General Denise Hart. Ionia County Circuit Court Judge David Hoort presided over the trial.

Schrauben formerly co-owned Schrauben Lehman Funeral Homes in Portland and Ionia with Michael Lehman.

Schrauben was found guilty of 26 charges, including conducting a criminal enterprise, receiving racketeering proceeds from a criminal enterprise, uttering and publishing death certificates, forgery of death certificates, uttering and publishing checks, insurance/fraudulent acts, embezzling $1,000 to $20,000, embezzling $50,000 to $100,000 and embezzling $100,000 or more.

In addition, Hoort revoked bond for Schrauben, saying that he could have an incentive to leave the countyâ€™s jurisdiction, and wanted to ensure he was in the county for sentencing. The judge said Schrauben is expected to be sentenced in the next four to eight weeks.

One count of criminal enterprises - racketeering proceeds, a felony punishable by up to 20 years in prison and/or a $100,000 fine;

One count of conducting criminal enterprises, a felony punishable by up to 20 years in prison and/or a $100,000 fine;

Two counts of embezzlement, $100,000 or more, a felony punishable by up to 20 years in prison and/or a fine up to $50,000 or three times the amount embezzled, whichever is greater;

One count of embezzlement, $50,000 - $100,000, a felony punishable by up to 15 years in prison and/or a fine up to $25,000 or three times the amount embezzled, whichever is greater.

Five counts of embezzlement, $1,000 - $20,000, a felony punishable by up to 5 years in prison and/or a $10,000 fine;

Eight counts of uttering and publishing, a felony punishable by up to 14 years in prison;

Four counts of forgery, a felony punishable by up to 14 years in prison; and,

Four counts of insurance fraud, a felony punishable by up to 4 years in prison and a $50,000 fine.
