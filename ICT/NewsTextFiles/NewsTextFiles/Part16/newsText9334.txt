
The San Antonio Spurs (0-0) and the Oklahoma City Thunder (0-0) will clash tonight in San Antonio for Game 1 of the Western Conference Finals. The Spurs are coming off a semifinals series win over the Blazers 4-1 while the Thunder are coming off a 4-2 semifinals win over the Clippers.

This will mark the second time the Spurs and Thunder clashed for the West crown. The last time was in 2012 where OKC defeated the Spurs, 4-2. In addition, the Thunder swept the Spurs in the regular season 4-0 but will be taking on the Spurs without Serge Ibaka who is out for the postseason with a calf injury.

During the 2014 playoffs, Oklahoma City is 4-1 in games played after a loss.

The Thunder are 3-0 this postseason series when leading the series 3-2.

Through the first 13 games of the NBA playoffs, Kevin Durant is averaging 31.4 points per game.

In four games against the Thunder this season, Spurs’ Tony Parker averaged 20.5 points, 5.5 assists and 2.5 rebounds.

Spurs’ Kawhi Leonard played in three games against Oklahoma City and averaged 11.3 points, 4.3 rebounds, 2.0 assists and 2.7 steals.
