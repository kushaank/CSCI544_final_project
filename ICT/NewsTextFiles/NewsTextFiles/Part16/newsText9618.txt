
Trade ministers and officials from 12 participating countries in the Trans-Pacific Partnership (TPP) negotiation have converged for another round of talks, taking stock from the last ministerial meeting held in February.

February's gathering saw ministers agreeing on a majority of the landing zones identified at an earlier December meeting.

Malaysia is represented in the two-day ministerial meeting by International Trade and Industry Minister Datuk Seri Mustapa Mohamed (pic), while the other countries are represented by their chief negotiators.

Those attending are Singapore Trade and Industry Minister Lim Hng Kiang, Australian Trade and Investment Minister Andrew Robb, Japanese Economic and Fiscal Policy Minister Akira Amari, New Zealand Trade Minister Tim Groser, Peru Foreign Commerce and Tourism Minister Magali Silva Verlande-Alvarez.

The meeting, hosted by Singapore, also saw the attendance of Ambassador Michael Froman from the United States.

Prior to the meeting, chief negotiators and subject matter experts met in Vietnam from May 11 to May 17.

Among the issues discussed in Ho Chi Minh City included goods and services market access packages, legal and institutional issues, intellectual property, environment, textiles and financial services.
