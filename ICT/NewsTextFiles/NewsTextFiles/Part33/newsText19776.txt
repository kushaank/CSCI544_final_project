
A solemn remembrance service has been held by the staff of the Flag Staff House, the seat of Government for the late Chief Presidential Adviser Paul Victor Obeng who passed on last Saturday after short illness in Accra.The service on Monday was also attended by clergy from the Roman Catholic Church in Ghana.

The pensive ceremony was officiated by an Irish Priest, Father Andrew Campbell who is currently the Parish Priest of the Christ The King Catholic Church in Accra where the late presidential adviser worshipped.

In his short sermon, Father Campbell described late Obeng as his personal friend who contributed to the development of the church in Ghana. He said Obeng was a humble man who reached out to many people.
