
Rome, May 19 - Premier Matteo Renzi said Monday he was confident Italy will be a bigger player in the EU after Sunday's European elections.       "I'm convinced that Italy will be increasingly more of a protagonist in Europe from May 26," he said in a press conference after meeting Polish Prime Minister Donald Tusk.      Italy takes over the rotating presidency of the European Union in July.      Renzi, who has called for major reform of the EU to combat growing Euroskepticism, added that he was upbeat about how his centre-left Democratic Party (PD) will do on Sunday.      The PD is top in most polls, but the anti-establishment 5-Star Movement (M5S) of comedian-cum-politician Beppe Grillo, who are second in most surveys, are confident they can come first with a late surge.      "I'm not worried, I'm very optimistic," said Renzi, who unseated his PD colleague Enrico Letta in February to become Italy's youngest premier at 39 and has embarked on an ambitious reform programme.      "There is a growing climate of hope and confidence and this needs concrete responses".
