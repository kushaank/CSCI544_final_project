
IN A SUPREME court in India on 16 May, six convicts - five awarded the death sentence and the other life imprisonment - were acquitted over not having sufficient evidence to convict them of any involvement in a small-arms and grenade on the Akshardham temple in the city of Gandhinagar in India's Gujarat state on 24 September 2002, where 30 people were killed and 80 others were wounded, The Times of India reported.
