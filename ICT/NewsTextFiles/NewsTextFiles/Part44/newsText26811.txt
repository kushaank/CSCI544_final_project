
“Following completion of the spring military training programs that involved troop redeployment and exercises at test grounds in Rostov, Belgorod and Bryansk regions, Mr Putin sent the order to the Defense Minister for troops that took part in these exercises to return to their usual garrisons and continue their training at neighboring test grounds,” a statement on the Kremlin website said.

The statement began by saying that Russia appeals for the immediate halt of punitive operations and use of force, withdrawal of troops, and resolution of the various problems through peaceful means alone.

“President Vladimir Putin welcomes the first contacts between Kiev and supporters of federalisation, seeking to establish direct dialogue in which all parties concerned should take part,” the statement said.

Ukraine’s Donetsk and Lugansk regions held referendums on May 11, where a majority of voters supported self-rule. The referendums were held after Crimea voted to join Russia on March 18. The validity of the referendums has not been recognized by the West, and voting fraud and voter intimidation has been alleged.
