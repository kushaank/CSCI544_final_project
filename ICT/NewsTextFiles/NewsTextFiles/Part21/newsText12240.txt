
The rogue trader at the heart of the biggest financial scandal in French  history was behind bars today after a final plea to President Hollande fell  on deaf ears.

Jérôme Kerviel, who brought Société Générale to its knees when he lost almost  €5 billion, was taken to Nice prison in southern France to begin a  three-year term.

The 37-year-old hairdresser’s son from Brittany was arrested in the early  hours after crossing into France from Italy after a threat by Paris to issue  a European arrest warrant for him.
