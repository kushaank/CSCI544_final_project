
KATHMANDU: India's would-be prime minister Narendra Modi, who represents the pro-Hindu Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP), has assured to strengthen bilateral relation between Nepal and India in various spheres.

"Nepal is an old & deeply valued friend. We are committed to strengthening our relations with Nepal in various spheres," Modi's latest tweet posted 13 hours ago reads.

Modi also thanked President Dr Ram Baran Yadav, Prime Minister Sushil Koirala and former prime minister and UCPN-Maoist leader Dr Baburam Bhattarai for their wishes to his election victory.

Posting the most resounding victory in India's election history in last 30 years, BJP alone won at least 275 of 543 seats in the Parliament. Modi was victorious from India's Varanasi and Vadodara.

Earlier yesterday, Prime Minister Sushil Koirala had called up Modi and congratulated him for the BJP's victory in the Indian general elections.

The Nepali executive head has invited Modi to visit Nepal after the latter assumes his office in New Delhi.
