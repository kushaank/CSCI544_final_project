
MAS last Thursday reported its biggest quarterly loss in more than two years, hit by a sharp drop in passenger traffic after what it called the "dramatic impact" of the MH370 tragedy.

The net loss in the first quarter expanded to 443.4 million ringgit in the first quarter from 278.8 million ringgit in the same period last year, it said. For the full year, analysts are forecasting a net loss of around 1 billion ringgit, a slight improvement from 1.17 billion in 2013.
