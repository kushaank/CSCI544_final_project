
China’s benchmark money-market rate declined to a two-week low on speculation policy makers will ensure ample fund supply amid signs a slowdown in the world’s second-largest economy is deepening.

New-home prices in April rose in 44 of the 70 cities tracked by the government, compared with 56 cities in March, the National Bureau of Statistics reported yesterday. Gains were recorded in the fewest metropolitan areas since October 2012 even as developers offered discounts and the economy slowed, prompting the easing of property curbs in some areas.

The seven-day repurchase agreement rate, a gauge of funding availability, dropped five basis points to 3.10 percent in Shanghai, according to a fixing from the National Interbank Funding Center. That’s the lowest since May 6. The rate fell 108 basis points, or 1.08 percentage point, this month. One-year interest-rate swaps, the fixed cost to receive the floating seven-day repo, rose two basis points to 3.76 percent, data compiled by Bloomberg show.

The People’s Bank of China gauged demand for 14- and 28-day repurchase contracts this week, according to a trader at a primary dealer required to bid at the auctions. The monetary authority injected a net 44 billion yuan ($7 billion) into the financial system last week.

Ten-year government bonds yielded 4.19 percent, compared with 4.20 percent at the end of last week, data from the National Interbank Funding Center shows.
