
CHENNAI: Veteran nuclear scientist and Padma Bhushan recipient, N Srinivasan (84), died on Sunday after suffering a cardiac arrest. He is survived by his wife, daughter and son, an official release here said.

According to the release, Srinivasan joined the Department of Atomic Energy in 1953 and was the design engineer for the plutonium plant at Trombay.

“He was the first Project Director of the Reactor Research Centre at Kalpakkam and was responsible for establishing various programmes related to fast reactors,” the release added.

He was awarded the Padma Bhushan in 2000. He also recieved the Lifetime Achievement Award of DAE for excellence in science and technology in 2009.
