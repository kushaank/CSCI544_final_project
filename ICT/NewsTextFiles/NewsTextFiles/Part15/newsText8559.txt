NORTH KINGSTOWN, R.I. (WPRI) — A unique ship cruised into the port of Davisville in Quonset on Monday.

NYK’s Auriga Leader is a massive vessel. It’s 200 feet long, and arrived in Rhode Island to deliver a shipment of more than 700 vehicles. While most ships of its size are fueled by oil, the Auriga Leader is partially powered by the sun.

300 solar panels line the top of the one of a kind ship, providing electricity to the entire vessel.

The Auriga Leader’s stop in Rhode Island will be brief. Its next stop is Boston, before sailing back across the Atlantic to Europe.
