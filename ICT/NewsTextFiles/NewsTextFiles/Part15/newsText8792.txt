HUNTINGTON, W.Va. (WSAZ) -- Huntington Police say preliminary results show a fatal crash may have been caused when a driver had a heart attack.

The crash happened about 7:30 a.m. Monday on Route 60 at the intersection of Washington Boulevard.

Police say Russell Franklin Wills, 42, of Hurricane, died in the crash.

They say according to the death certificate, Wills died from a heart attack.

Five vehicles were involved in the crash.

They are still working on their investigation.

UPDATE 5/19/14 @ 3:15 p.m.

HUNTINGTON, W.Va. (WSAZ) -- A man from Hurricane died in a crash Monday morning on Route 60 in Huntington.

The crash happened about 7:30 a.m. at the intersection of Route 60 and Washington Boulevard.

Huntington Police say five vehicles were involved in the crash.

They say Russell Franklin Wills, 42, of Hurricane, died in the crash. Lt. Levi Livingston with HPD says Wills was driving a silver Toyota Camry.

Police say two others were taken to the hospital with non life-threatening injuries.

The cause of the accident is still under investigation.

Route 60 was closed for several hours while crews cleared the scene. It reopened around 11:30 a.m.

UPDATE 5/19/14 @ 11:24 a.m.

HUNTINGTON, W.Va. (WSAZ) -- Route 60 has reopened in Huntington following a fatal crash Monday morning.

The road was shut down for nearly four hours in the area of Washington Boulevard.

The crash involving a moving truck and four cars happened about 7:30 a.m.

Huntington Police Capt. Hank Dial tells WSAZ.com that one person died in the crash. Two others were injured and transported to the hospital.

The cause of the crash has not been released.

No names have been released at this time.
