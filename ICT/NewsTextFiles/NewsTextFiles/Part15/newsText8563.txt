KENT COUNTY, MI -- A 10-year-old and her parents, along with another adult male, are hospitalized in Grand Rapids Monday morning following a weekend crash near Cedar Springs.

Sheriff's authorities said Adam Brace, 29, of Cedar Springs was westbound on 15 Mile Road in a Grand Prix around 3:45 p.m. Sunday when he disregarded a stop sign at Myers Lake Avenue.

Brace's vehicle collided with a silver 2000 Dodge Caravan driven by Lowell resident Alan Kenyon, 53. Kenyon had been southbound on Myers Lake Avenue. Both vehicles landed in a ditch along the side of the road May 18.

Alan Kenyon and his wife, Christine Kenyon, were listed in serious condition Monday morning at Spectrum Health Butterworth Hospital. The couple's daughter, Abigail Kenyon, was listed as fair at Helen DeVos Children's Hospital.

Brace also was in serious condition at Butterworth Hospital.

Christine Kenyon was transported from the crash scene in Courtland Township by an Aero Med helicopter.

Sunday night marked at least the third time in four days Aero Med was called to a serious injury crash in Kent County.
