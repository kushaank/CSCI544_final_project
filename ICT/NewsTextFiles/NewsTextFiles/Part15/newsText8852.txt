In 2007, Congress passed the Energy Independence and Security Act, which created the Renewable Fuel Standard. Essentially, what that did was to mandate that, by 2009, all gasoline in the U.S. contain 10 percent renewable fuels.

The most common of these blended biofuels is ethanol. Essentially grain alcohol, ethanol as been mass produced mostly using corn, which corn farmers think is a great idea since it provides a sure market for their crop; others view it as problematic. One frequent complaint is that with so many people going hungry in the world, we ought not to be putting a valuable food crop into car fuel tanks.
