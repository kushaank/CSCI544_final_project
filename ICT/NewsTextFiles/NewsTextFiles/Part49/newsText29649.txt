
A Pacific media watchdog says budget cuts to the Australian Broadcasting Corporation send a chilling message to the region.

The cuts will mean the end of the Australia Network television service and a paring back of Radio Australia's services.

The Pacific Freedom Forum says the message that sends, is that people in the Pacific's access to independent news and information does not matter to Australia anymore.

The PFF Chair, Titi Gabi says the ABC is vital to the region, where there is little true media freedom.
