
Expectations that the Bank of England will intervene next month to cool the  housing market have surfaced as some of the world’s other major central  banks have taken similar action.

Earlier this month, Sweden’s regulators made it more expensive for lenders to  issue mortgages by increasing the amount of loss-absorbing capital they must  hold against home loans.

The Bank has the power to do something similar, under the principle of  “popping not mopping” as Andy Haldane, executive director for financial  stability, put it.
