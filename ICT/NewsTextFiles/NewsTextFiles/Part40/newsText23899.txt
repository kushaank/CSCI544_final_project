Just days before banana giant Chiquita announced its intent to move the company's shipping operations from Gulfport, Miss., to New Orleans, Popular Science published an unsettling article on the future of the world's largest fruit crop. The story -- "Has The End Of The Banana Arrived?" -- posits that a banana fungus commonly known as Panama disease poses a serious threat to the global banana supply, which generates $8 billion each year and serves as the primary source of calories for 400 million people.

A recent study found that the disease has infested a 3,500-acre banana farm in Mozambique, where fruit is now destined to wilt and rot, the article said. Once limited to parts of Southeast Asia, the fungus has spread to China and Australia, causing one plant pathologist in the story to note that the recent outbreak "endangers banana crops beyond Mozambique's borders."
