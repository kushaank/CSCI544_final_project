
As the world galvanizes against Islamist terrorist group Boko Haram's abduction of Nigerian school girls from Chibok, the  ANC women's league lit candles of support at the Nigerian embassy in Pretoria last night.

President of the ANC women's leagues Angie Motshekga, ANC-NEC members together with other organisations  lead a candle lit procession with 230 candles.

Motshekga said the candles," represent the number of the missing girls as a way of encouraging the mothers not to lose hope until they are home safe. The lighting will be done at the Nigerian embassy and we urge South Africans to join us and pray for safe return of the girls."

The ANCWL campaign will be an ongoing one .

She called on South Africans, Africans and people across the world to sign their social solidarity petition to unite behind the "BringBackOurGirls" campaign.

"The petition will be available on the ANC and ANC women's league websites and we will hand it over to Nigerian government," Motshekga  said.

From the  21st - 23rd May there will be picketing in selected public building led by Sesonke Men Sector and Men's Group in Action.

ANC women's league secretary general Sisisi Tolashe said: "We are part of the liberation movement that has been existing in the continent and Nigeria is a member of SADC hence it is our responsibility to intervene. This is also a reminder to us as a country because we have been there though our was not that hectic".

Young Communist League secretary Buti Manamela lashed out at the Nigerian government when he read a memorandum at the Nigerian High commission in Pretoria.
