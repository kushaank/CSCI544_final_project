PROVIDENCE, R.I. (AP/WPRI) — A Providence 14-year-old representing Rhode Island for the second time in the National Geographic Bee is off to a perfect start.

Max Levine, an eighth grader at Wheeler School, is competing against 53 other students in the 26th annual bee, which began Monday in Washington, D.C.

Max Levine, an 8th grader at The Wheeler School, is representing Rhode Island at the National Geographic Bee in Washington, DC.

According to a school spokeswoman, Levine received a perfect score in the preliminary round.

Levine tells The Providence Journal he has been studying online with some of the friends he met while competing in the bee in 2012.

Levine also has some first-hand experience with geography.  He says he’s traveled with his family to Europe, Central America, Israel and the Caribbean.
