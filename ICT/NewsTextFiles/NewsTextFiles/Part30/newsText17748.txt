
TOLEDO, Ohio (AP) — A decision preventing sediment dredged out of Cleveland’s harbor from being dumped into Lake Erie has satisfied those worried about it fouling fish and drinking water.

The shift in plans, though, won’t end the debate over what to do with tons of silt scooped out of northern Ohio’s bays and rivers so that cargo boats can navigate the waters.
