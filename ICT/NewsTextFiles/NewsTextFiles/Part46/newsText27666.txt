About one third of Alabama's future pension liability was unfunded as of fiscal year 2012, according to a survey of financial data conducted by the Pew Charitable Trusts.

According to Pew, Alabama had a liability of $42.5 billion in 2012. Of that total, roughly $14.4 billion was unfunded. And Alabama's funded ratio has decreased in each of the last two years, from 70 percent, to 67 percent, to 66 percent.
