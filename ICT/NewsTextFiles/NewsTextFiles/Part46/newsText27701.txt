
CHARLESTON, W.Va. (WSAZ) -- Charleston City Council on Monday approved a grant application to the National Endowment for the Arts to to help fund a sculpture at the Charleston Town Center Mall.

The grant is for partial funding of $10,000 for the sculpture to serve as a gateway to Charleston.

City Manager David Molgaard said the city is working to try to bring more people to the Charleston area.

"We have really been focusing on arts in Charleston," Molgaard said. "Something about the arts says that we are a vibrant city, and it attracts creative people, and it really makes it a place that people really want to be."

The total cost of the project is about $20,000. FestivALL is providing the other $10,000.
