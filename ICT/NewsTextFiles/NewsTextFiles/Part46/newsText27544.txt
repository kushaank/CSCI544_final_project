
RENO, Nev. (AP) — Three Nevada fish biologists are part of a team of scientists being honored by the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service for their work to try to help bring the Devils Hole pupfish back from the brink of extinction near Death Valley National Park.

Lee Simons, the federal agency's senior fish biologist in Las Vegas, and state biologists Kevin Guadalupe and Brandon Senger are on the team among five recognized nationally.
